EBOOK 2: PLANCHE MASTERY GUIDE
By Jacob Hanif — World Record Holder in Planche Variations
========================================

Phased Progression System:

PHASE 1: FOUNDATIONS (Tuck Planche)
- Start with planche leans on floor or parallettes
- Hands at waistline (centre of gravity)
- Maintain posterior pelvic tilt, scapular protraction and depression
- Elbows LOCKED at all times
- Hold 3-5 seconds, rest 4-5 minutes between sets
- Benchmark: 5 seconds clean tuck planche on elevation

PHASE 2: ADVANCED TUCK
- Support drills: advanced tuck to tuck transitions, deep leans, band protraction work
- Benchmark: 5 seconds of advanced tuck with stability and clean alignment

PHASE 3: LOW STRADDLE PLANCHE
- Straddle legs, maintain scapular protraction/depression and hollow body
- Lean forward in controlled manner until feet begin to lift
- Benchmark: 5 seconds clean low straddle

PHASE 4: STRADDLE PLANCHE
- Full straddle with aligned hips and shoulders
- Focus: controlled lean, locked elbows, protracted scapulae

PHASE 5: FULL PLANCHE
- Legs together, full extension
- Requires mastery of all previous phases

Key Coaching Cues:
- "Hands at your waistline — that's your centre of gravity"
- "Lock the elbows, always"
- "Protract and depress the scapulae"
- "Posterior pelvic tilt — no banana back"
- "4-5 minutes rest between sets minimum"
- "Don't chase longer holds — chase smarter progressions"

Jacob's #1 Rule: "The biggest mistake I see is the obsession with longer holds instead of smarter progressions. Once most people unlock a progression, they grind it to death instead of moving to the next phase."

========================================
TRAINING PHILOSOPHY
========================================

- Mechanical tension over metabolic stress for strength skills
- Brief isometric holds at mid-range for connective tissue adaptation
- Collagen synthesis in tendons reduces injury risk
- Frequency and micro-periodisation over volume
- Flexibility and strength are partners — muscle length and strength work together
- Eccentric loading strengthens connective tissue
- Active recovery maintains fascial pliability

INJURY MANAGEMENT (Personal Experience)

Jacob's herniated discs (L4-L5, L5-S1):

- Emphasis on spinal safety, neutral spine, joint centration
